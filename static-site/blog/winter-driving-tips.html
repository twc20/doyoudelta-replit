Winter Driving in the Four Corners: Essential Tips

By Delta Tire Team • August 28, 2025

Winter in the Four Corners region brings unique driving challenges. From mountain passes to high desert plateaus, New Mexico and Arizona winter weather demands preparation and caution. Here's your comprehensive guide to safe winter driving.

Prepare Your Vehicle Before Winter

1. Check Your Tires

Proper tires are your first line of defense in winter conditions. All-season tires work for most Four Corners driving, but winter tires provide superior traction in snow and ice. Ensure your tires have adequate tread depth—at least 6/32" for winter driving.

2. Test Your Battery

Cold weather is hard on batteries. A battery that starts fine in summer may fail in freezing temperatures. Get your battery tested before winter arrives—free testing available at all Delta Tire locations.

3. Check Fluids

Ensure antifreeze is at proper levels and concentration. Check windshield washer fluid and use winter formula that won't freeze. Consider switching to winter-weight oil if you live in high-elevation areas.

4. Inspect Wipers and Defroster

Replace worn wiper blades and ensure your defroster works properly. You need clear visibility in winter conditions.

Winter Emergency Kit Essentials

Always carry these items during winter months:

  • Blankets and warm clothes - Essential if stranded in remote areas
  • Flashlight and extra batteries - Winter darkness comes early
  • Ice scraper and snow brush - Must-haves for mountain driving
  • Jumper cables or battery pack - Cold kills batteries
  • Small shovel - For digging out of snow
  • Sand or kitty litter - Provides traction on ice
  • Non-perishable snacks and water - In case you're stranded
  • First aid kit - Basic emergency supplies
  • Phone charger - Keep your phone powered

Safe Winter Driving Techniques

Slow Down

Speed limits are set for ideal conditions. Reduce your speed in snow, ice, and low visibility. Allow extra time to reach your destination.

Increase Following Distance

In dry conditions, maintain a 3-second following distance. In winter, increase to 8-10 seconds. This gives you time to stop safely on slippery roads.

Brake Gently

Slamming brakes on ice causes skids. Apply steady, gentle pressure. If you have ABS brakes, press firmly and let the system do its job. Without ABS, pump the brakes gently.

Accelerate Smoothly

Gentle acceleration prevents wheel spin on ice and snow. Too much power breaks traction and causes loss of control.

Know How to Handle Skids

If your rear wheels skid, steer in the direction you want to go (into the skid). Don't slam on brakes. If front wheels skid, take your foot off the gas until traction returns.

Four Corners Specific Considerations

Mountain Passes

Routes like US-160 over Wolf Creek Pass and I-40 near Flagstaff can become treacherous. Check road conditions before traveling. Carry chains if required.

Black Ice

High desert areas experience black ice—nearly invisible ice on roads. Be especially cautious on bridges, overpasses, and shaded areas in early morning.

Elevation Changes

Weather can change dramatically with elevation. You might leave Gallup in sunshine and hit snow 30 minutes later in the mountains.

Remote Areas

Many Four Corners roads are remote. Cell service may be limited. Tell someone your route and expected arrival time when traveling in winter.

When Not to Drive

Sometimes the safest choice is staying home. Avoid driving if:

  • Roads are closed or travel is not advised
  • Visibility is severely limited
  • Your vehicle isn't properly equipped
  • You're not comfortable with winter driving
