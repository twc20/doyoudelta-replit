How Wheel Alignment Saves You Money on Tires

By Delta Tire Team • August 22, 2025

Proper wheel alignment is one of the most cost-effective maintenance services you can perform. A simple alignment can extend your tire life by thousands of miles and save you hundreds of dollars. Here's how.

What Is Wheel Alignment?

Wheel alignment (also called tire alignment) adjusts the angles of your wheels to manufacturer specifications. It's not about aligning the tires or wheels themselves, but rather adjusting the suspension components that control how your tires contact the road.

Three angles are adjusted during an alignment:

  • Toe - Whether tires point inward or outward when viewed from above
  • Camber - The inward or outward tilt when viewed from the front
  • Caster - The steering axis angle when viewed from the side

How Misalignment Destroys Tires

Uneven Tread Wear

Misaligned wheels cause tires to drag across the pavement at an angle rather than rolling straight. This creates rapid, uneven wear patterns:

  • One-sided wear - One edge wears faster than the other (camber problem)
  • Feathering - Tread ribs wear at an angle (toe problem)
  • Center or edge wear - Often indicates improper inflation, but can be worsened by alignment issues

Accelerated Wear Rate

Even minor misalignment significantly accelerates tire wear. Studies show that misalignment can reduce tire life by 25-50%. On tires rated for 60,000 miles, that's 15,000-30,000 miles of lost tread life.

The Real Cost of Misalignment

Let's look at the numbers:

Example: Family SUV

  • Tire set cost: $800 (4 tires at $200 each)
  • Expected life: 60,000 miles with proper alignment
  • Cost per mile: $0.013

With Misalignment (25% reduction in life):

  • Actual tire life: 45,000 miles
  • Cost per mile: $0.018
  • Extra cost over 60,000 miles: $267

Cost of alignment: $89-$129

Net savings: $138-$178

That's money saved every time you need tires—which for most drivers is every 3-5 years.

When to Get an Alignment

With New Tires

Always get an alignment when installing new tires. This protects your investment and ensures even wear from day one. Many tire shops include free alignment checks with new tire purchases.

After Hitting a Pothole or Curb

Road impacts can knock wheels out of alignment. New Mexico and Arizona roads have their share of potholes, especially after winter weather. If you hit a significant pothole or curb, get your alignment checked.

When You Notice These Signs

  • Vehicle pulls to one side
  • Steering wheel is off-center when driving straight
  • Uneven tire wear
  • Steering wheel vibration
  • Vehicle feels unstable on the highway

Regular Maintenance Schedule

We recommend checking alignment every 10,000 miles or annually, whichever comes first. This catches small problems before they destroy tires.

Beyond Tire Savings: Other Benefits

Improved Fuel Economy

Misaligned wheels create resistance, making your engine work harder. Proper alignment can improve fuel economy by 1-2 MPG. Over a year, that's another $50-$100 in savings.

Better Handling and Safety

Proper alignment improves vehicle stability, steering response, and braking. This is especially important in emergency situations where every bit of control matters.

Reduced Suspension Wear

Misalignment puts extra stress on suspension components, causing premature wear to ball joints, tie rod ends, and control arm bushings. Proper alignment extends the life of these expensive parts.

What to Expect During an Alignment

At Delta Tire, our computerized alignment process includes:

  1. Pre-alignment inspection - We check for worn suspension parts that could affect alignment
  2. Precision measurement - Computerized equipment measures current alignment angles
  3. Adjustment - Technicians adjust angles to manufacturer specifications
