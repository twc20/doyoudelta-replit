When Should You Replace Your Tires?

1. Tread Depth

When tread depth reaches 2/32". Use the penny test - if you can see Lincoln's full head, it's time for new tires.

2. Age

Tires older than 6 years should be inspected. Replace at 10 years regardless of tread depth due to rubber degradation.

3. Visible Damage

Cracks, bulges, blisters, or uneven wear indicate it's time for replacement. Don't risk a blowout.
