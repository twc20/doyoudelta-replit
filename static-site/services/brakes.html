Brake Warning Signs

Never ignore these symptoms—they indicate your brakes need attention

Squealing or Grinding

High-pitched squealing means your brake pads are worn. Grinding indicates metal-on-metal contact—get service immediately.

Soft or Spongy Pedal

A brake pedal that feels soft or goes to the floor indicates air in the brake lines or a fluid leak. This is a serious safety issue.

Vibration or Pulsing

Vibration when braking usually means warped rotors. This reduces braking effectiveness and should be addressed promptly.

Pulling to One Side

If your vehicle pulls left or right when braking, you may have a stuck caliper or uneven brake wear.

Warning Light

The brake warning light indicates low fluid, worn pads, or an ABS system issue. Don't ignore it—get it checked.

Burning Smell

A burning smell after heavy braking or when driving indicates overheated brakes or a stuck caliper.
