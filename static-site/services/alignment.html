Wheel Alignment Service

Precision alignment for better handling and longer tire life

Computerized Wheel Alignment

Proper wheel alignment is essential for even tire wear, optimal fuel economy, and safe vehicle handling. Our computerized alignment equipment provides precise measurements and adjustments to manufacturer specifications.

Whether you've hit a pothole, installed new tires, or notice your vehicle pulling to one side, a professional alignment can save you money and improve your driving experience.

Alignment Benefits:

  • Extended tire life (up to 12,000 miles)
  • Improved fuel economy
  • Better vehicle handling
  • Reduced steering effort
  • Straight steering wheel
  • Safer driving experience

Signs You Need an Alignment

Uneven Tire Wear

If one side of your tires is wearing faster than the other, or you see feathering on the tread, you need an alignment check.

Vehicle Pulls

Your car pulls to the left or right when driving on a straight, level road. This is a clear sign of alignment issues.

Crooked Steering Wheel

When driving straight, your steering wheel is off-center or at an angle. This indicates your wheels aren't properly aligned.

Vibration

Steering wheel vibration at highway speeds can indicate alignment problems or other suspension issues.

After Impact

Hit a pothole or curb? Even minor impacts can knock your wheels out of alignment. Get it checked.

New Tires

Always get an alignment with new tires. It ensures even wear and protects your tire investment.

What We Adjust

Toe

The angle of the tires when viewed from above. Incorrect toe causes rapid, uneven tire wear.

Camber

The inward or outward tilt of the tire when viewed from the front. Affects tire contact with the road.

Caster

The angle of the steering axis when viewed from the side. Affects steering stability and cornering.
